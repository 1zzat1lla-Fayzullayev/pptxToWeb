How HydrAttack works

HydrAttack has an unique algorithm that enables rapid and effective detection of external threats. The algorithm consists of more than 30 constantly updated core nodes for information gathering and analysis, which summarize and complement each other

The system is divided into two main streams: passive and active scanning, which detect unmanaged and managed threats, respectively

STEP 1

In the passive stream, we obtain a list of employees through LinkedIn and search engines

STEP 2

Next, we analyze "leaked" credentials like usernames and passwords. From this data, we deduce that if your name is John White, then your account follows a j.white@company.com pattern

STEP 3

Than, we combine the names from step 1 with the pattern from step 2 to generate account list

STEP 4

In the active stream, during the scanning process, we find the login page for the corporate Outlook OWA mail service

STEP 5

We begin brute-forcing the Outlook using the obtained and generated credentials
